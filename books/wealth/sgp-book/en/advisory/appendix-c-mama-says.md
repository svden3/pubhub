# Appendix C: Complete Mama Quotes

> Mama ran a restaurant in Baton Rouge. She never went to college. But her words are more useful than any investment book.

---

## About Investing

> "Don't put all your eggs in one basket."

The simplest version of diversification.

> "Bubbles are like soda. Feels great drinking it, embarrassing when you burp."

Every generation has its bubbles. Be careful while enjoying — bubbles always pop.

> "If you can't understand what someone is saying, they're either a genius or a con artist. Geniuses are rare."

The common thread of Enron, Theranos, FTX: their business models couldn't be explained clearly.

> "Trees that survive winter have the deepest roots."

The crises of 2000, 2008, 2020 all passed. Survivors are stronger.

> "When everyone says something can't happen, it's usually already happening."

"House prices won't fall," "Lehman won't fail" — these words came true shortly after being spoken.

> "Never trust any prediction that contains the word 'never.'"

Nothing is forever.

---

## About Life

> "Slow and steady wins the race."

What Mama said when I left for Silicon Valley. Compound interest needs time. Careers need accumulation. No shortcuts.

> "Not every battle is worth fighting. Sometimes the smartest thing is to walk away."

Leaving a place that doesn't fit you isn't failure, it's wisdom.

> "Some places aren't right for you. That doesn't mean you're wrong. It means that place is wrong."

The DataSphere experience taught me this.

> "The tree you plant may not be the tree you sit under. But at least you planted."

About cross-training those young people. Giving may not pay back, but giving itself has meaning.

> "Slow is fine, but not so slow you die of old age."

About my relationship with Sarah. On important things, don't drag too long.

---

## About Common Sense

> "Running a business is like making Gumbo — temperature has to be right, ingredients have to be good, and most importantly — you need patience."

Patience is the foundation of everything.

> "When opportunities come, they usually don't look like opportunities."

The Google offer in 2004, Kevin going to Facebook in 2004. No one thought they were opportunities at the time.

> "When you buy stocks because of a picture of a dog, you're not investing, you're buying lottery tickets."

About Dogecoin. Emojis are not investment reasons.

> "When someone says they're making money to help others, first see how they spend their own money."

About SBF and "effective altruism." Actions are more real than slogans.

> "Confidence and ability are two different things. Scammers are usually very confident, because they know they're scamming you."

About Elizabeth Holmes and Theranos. Confidence doesn't equal ability.

> "Banks are like doctors — you think they're reliable, until you really need them."

Silicon Valley Bank collapsed in 2023. Nothing is absolutely safe.

> "Tools won't replace people, but people who can use tools will replace people who can't."

About AI. Learn to use new tools, don't fear them.

---

## About Education

> "Give a child a fish, you feed them for a day. Give a child money, you spoil them. Teach a child to manage money, you prepare them for life."

Financial literacy education is more important than leaving an inheritance.

> "School teaches knowledge, family teaches character. A good school is a bonus, not a necessity."

About the public vs private debate.

> "Money can buy good education, but the best teacher is free — that's you."

Parental presence is more important than tuition.

> "Son, I didn't give you an Ivy education, but I gave you the right values. That's worth more than any degree."

Mama's summary on education.

---

## The Last Quote

> "Son, are you sure you're investing, not gambling?"

Before every investment decision, ask yourself this question.
