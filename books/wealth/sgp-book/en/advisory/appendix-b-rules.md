# Appendix B: Silicon Jim's 10 Iron Rules

> "25 years of wisdom, condensed into 10 rules."

---

## Iron Rule 1: Diversify

> **Don't put all your eggs in one basket, especially the basket where you work.**

- No single stock more than 10%
- Don't buy your company's stock
- Stocks + Bonds + Cash

**Mike made this mistake twice. Don't you make it.**

---

## Iron Rule 2: Safety First

> **Save six months of living expenses before thinking about investing.**

- Emergency fund in an accessible account
- Don't use this money for investing
- This is your safety cushion

**No safety cushion, no battlefield.**

---

## Iron Rule 3: Circle of Competence

> **If you can't understand it, don't invest in it.**

- Can't explain in three sentences, don't buy
- Too complex usually means too risky
- Big names invested ≠ Safe

**Enron, Theranos, FTX — all were "innovative."**

---

## Iron Rule 4: Contrarian Thinking

> **Be greedy when others are fearful, fearful when others are greedy.**

- When markets crash, consider buying more
- When markets are frenzied, stay alert
- But first, you need cash

**Those who bought in 2008 made 4x.**

---

## Iron Rule 5: Simplicity Is King

> **When in doubt, buy index funds.**

- VOO / VTI / SPY
- Expense ratio < 0.1%
- Buy, hold, don't touch

**Boring makes money.**

---

## Iron Rule 6: Automate

> **Take decision-making power away from emotions.**

- DCA monthly, automated
- Don't watch news, don't time the market
- Set rules, then follow them

**System > Impulse.**

---

## Iron Rule 7: Holding Is Key

> **Buying is the apprentice's skill. Holding is the master's art.**

- Don't sell because it dropped
- Don't run because it went up
- Compound interest needs time

**16 years of holding, 4x return.**

---

## Iron Rule 8: Plan B

> **Never put your fate entirely in someone else's hands.**

- Savings: 6-12 months living expenses
- Skills: Transferable abilities
- Network: People who can help you

**Stock options are icing on the cake. Savings are the lifeline.**

---

## Iron Rule 9: Time Is Most Precious

> **Money is a tool, time is the goal.**

- Use money to buy time, don't trade time for money
- Financial freedom = Freedom to choose
- Compound interest needs time, give it time

**Sarah retired at 48 to paint. That's being wealthy.**

---

## Iron Rule 10: Listen to Mama

> **Common sense beats cleverness.**

- Simple rules, followed long-term
- Don't try to be too "clever"
- The most complex problems usually have the simplest answers

**Mama never went to college, but her advice is the most useful.**
