## Sun Tzu's Five Keys to Victory

| Key to Victory | Original Meaning | Investment Mapping |

## Graham's Five Stock Selection Criteria

Benjamin Graham's criteria from *The Intelligent Investor* perfectly align with Sun Tzu's five keys:

| Key to Victory | Graham Criterion | Specific Metric |
|----------------|------------------|-----------------|
| Know when to fight | **Reasonable Valuation** | P/E < 15, P/B < 1.5 |

## I. Know When to Fight ‚Äî Reasonable Valuation

### Original Text

> "He will win who knows when to fight and when not to fight."

### Graham's Wisdom

> "Buy well, and you're halfway to success."

### Valuation Standards

| Metric | Graham Standard | Aggressive | Conservative |
|--------|-----------------|------------|--------------|
| **P/E Ratio** | < 15 | < 20 | < 12 |
| **P/B Ratio** | < 1.5 | < 2.0 | < 1.0 |
| **P/E √ó P/B** | < 22.5 | < 30 | < 15 |
| **Dividend Yield** | > 2/3 AAA bond yield | > 2% | > 3% |

### When NOT to Fight

Never buy in these situations:

| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| P/E > 25 | Overvalued, insufficient margin |
| P/B > 3 | Excessive market expectations |
| Can't understand business | "Never invest in what you don't understand" |
| Market euphoria | Possible top |

## IV. Prepared vs Unprepared ‚Äî Financial Strength

### Original Text

> "He will win who, prepared himself, waits to take the enemy unprepared."

### Graham's Wisdom

> "The first layer of margin of safety is a strong balance sheet."

### Financial Strength Standards

| Metric | Graham Standard | Meaning |
|--------|-----------------|---------|
| **Current Ratio** | > 2 | Strong short-term solvency |
| **Quick Ratio** | > 1 | Adequate liquidity |
| **Debt Ratio** | < 50% | Long-term financial safety |
| **Interest Coverage** | > 3x | Manageable debt burden |

### Three Layers of "Prepared"

```
Layer 1: Cash Buffer
‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ Covers 6-12 months of operating expenses

Layer 2: Asset Cushion
‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ Liquid assets > Short-term liabilities

Layer 3: Credit Backup
‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ Unused bank credit facilities
```

### Case: 2008 Financial Crisis

| Company | 2008 Financial Status | Result |
|---------|----------------------|--------|
| **Apple** | $25B cash, zero debt | Sailed through, quick recovery |
| **Lehman** | 30:1 leverage, short-term debt dependent | Bankruptcy |
| **Berkshire** | $20B+ cash | Bottom-fished Goldman Sachs |

**Lesson**: "Prepared vs unprepared" ‚Äî prepared companies survived.

## V. Able General, No Interference ‚Äî Earnings Stability

### Original Text

> "He will win whose generals are able and not interfered with by the sovereign."

### Graham's Wisdom

> "A stable earnings record indicates lasting competitive advantage."

### Earnings Stability Standards

| Metric | Graham Standard | Advanced Standard |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Profitable Years** | No losses in 10 years | 20+ years profitable |
| **Earnings Growth** | Positive | > Inflation + GDP |
| **ROE Stability** | No wild swings | Std Dev < 5% |
| **Gross Margin Trend** | Stable or rising | Not declining |

### Earnings Quality Assessment

| Dimension | Good Signal | Warning Signal |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Cash Conversion** | Operating CF ‚â• Net Income | Operating CF < Net Income |
| **Revenue Quality** | Stable core business | Dependent on non-recurring items |
| **Receivables** | Turnover improving | Turnover declining |
| **Inventory** | Stable turnover | Significant buildup |

### Case: Kodak vs Johnson & Johnson

| Dimension | Kodak (1990s-2000s) | J&J |
|-----------|---------------------|-----|
| Consecutive Profits | Started losing post-2000 | 100+ years profitable |
| Gross Margin | Declining yearly | Stable at 65%+ |
| Cash Flow | Deteriorating | Consistently strong |
| Outcome | 2012 bankruptcy | Continuous dividend growth |
