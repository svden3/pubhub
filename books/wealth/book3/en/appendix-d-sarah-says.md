# Appendix D: Complete Sarah Quotes

> Sarah worked at Google for 16 years. Retired at 48 to paint. Her investment advice is more reliable than any financial expert.

---

## About Investing

> "Never use your salary to buy your company's stock. You've already bet your human capital there. Don't bet your financial capital too."

Mike heard this twice and still didn't learn. Don't make the same mistake.

> "See, this is the point of diversification. Your human capital went to zero, but your financial capital is still there."

Said when NeuralMind shut down in 2011. Options went to zero, but index funds remained.

> "The world isn't ending. Fear is the best buy signal."

Said during the 2008 financial crisis. She told me to buy index funds. That investment grew 4x.

> "Be greedy when others are fearful. But first you need the capital to be greedy — cash."

The prerequisite for contrarian investing is having money. Without cash reserves, you can't seize opportunities.

> "When you don't know what to buy, buy the whole market."

About index funds. Can't pick stocks? Just buy VOO.

> "The hardest part of investing isn't buying. It's holding."

Said when the market hit bottom in 2009. Many people sold at the lowest point and missed the recovery.

---

## About Selling

> "Regret is the investor's worst enemy. Once you decide, don't look back."

In 2004 I didn't go to Google. She said don't regret, look forward.

> "You can't catch every opportunity. What matters is catching the one that belongs to you."

Missing Facebook, missing Google, doesn't matter. What matters is catching AI.

> "You always buy at the top and sell at the bottom. That's called chasing highs and selling lows."

Said to Mike. From Enron to Lehman, he entered at the peak every time.

---

## About Career

> "In your career, always have a Plan B. Don't put your fate entirely in someone else's hands."

Said after DataSphere. Savings, skills, network are real security.

> "Stock options are just icing on the cake. Real security comes from the number in your bank account."

Don't pin your hopes on options. Most options end up worthless.

> "Living well is the best revenge."

About leaving a bad company. Don't waste energy hating people. Put your energy into yourself.

---

## About Financial Freedom

> "Real wealth is time, not money. Money can buy time, but time can't be bought back."

Said at the café in 2025. She retired at 48 to paint. That's being truly wealthy.

> "Silicon Valley's biggest secret is: the really rich people aren't founders, they're early employees who held their stock long-term."

Founders dilute through fundraising. Early employees get options, hold after IPO. Compound interest. Time. Patience.

> "Google stock is enough for me to live three lifetimes. I want to paint now."

The definition of financial freedom: being able to do anything you want.

> "Find a good company, stay a long time, don't fidget."

Her investment strategy summary. Boring, but effective.

---

## About Judgment

> "If a company's financials require a genius to understand, they're either doing Nobel Prize-level innovation or prison-level fraud."

Her dad was a CPA. This was about Enron.

> "When everyone is using complex methods to explain a simple problem, the problem usually isn't what they say it is."

About the subprime crisis. Complexity is used to hide the truth.

> "This medical tech company has never published a single peer-reviewed paper."

About Theranos. A tech company without tech papers is suspicious.

> "Anything priced by emojis is not an investment, it's a gamble."

About Dogecoin and meme stocks.

> "He chases heat, not value. Always chasing, always bag-holding."

About why Tommy always loses money.

> "The common trait of scammers is: you can't understand what they're saying, but they're very confident."

A simple way to identify scammers.

---

## About Diversification

> "This is why you diversify your deposits. Never put all your money in one bank."

Said after Silicon Valley Bank collapsed in 2023.

> "Big names have money to gamble. You don't."

About those celebrities who invested in Theranos. They can afford to lose. You can't.

---

## The Last Quote

> "The difference between a bubble and a revolution is — a revolution eventually creates real value. But until then, they look exactly the same."

About the 2000 dot-com bubble and the 2024 AI boom. How to tell the difference: see if the company is actually making money.
